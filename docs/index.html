What was fixed (plain English)

On category pages like KEYTAG, choosing Best selling used to show products in the wrong order (low product IDs first). Your top seller Psyduck appeared on page 6 instead of page 1.

That is now fixed. When a customer picks a sort option, the whole category is sorted correctly in the database before the page is split into pages of 24 products — so page 1 always shows the real best sellers.

Live proof: Psyduck KEYTAG (74 sold) is now the first product on KEYTAG → Best selling.

How it works for your customers

  1. 1
    Visit any shop or category page (Shop, KEYTAG, COASTER, etc.). A Sort by dropdown appears above the product grid — no extra setup needed.
  2. 2
    Choose a sort option: Default, Best selling, Newest, Price low→high, Price high→low, or Top rated.
  3. 3
    The page reloads with ?orderby= in the URL. Products reorder instantly across the full catalog.
  4. 4
    Best selling uses real WooCommerce sales data (total items sold). You can see the sales count under each product (e.g. “74 items sold”).
Customer opens KEYTAG → Selects “Best selling” → URL adds ?orderby=popularity → WooCommerce sorts all keytags by sales → Page 1 shows Psyduck first

Technical summary (for your developer)

  • Root cause: Elementor Loop Grid “Current Query” bypassed WooCommerce posts_clauses SQL sorting.
  • Fix: v1.3.6 forces suppress_filters = false and hooks elementor/query/{id} on the WP_Query instance.
  • Deploy: WPCode snippet #11822 · Elementor template #8380 (ASC override removed) · WP Rocket cache purged.
  • Source code: github.com/mdhemalakanda/kabigon-shop-sort
